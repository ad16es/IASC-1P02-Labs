Madrid, the capital city of Spain has a population of 6.5 million people, making it the European Union's third largest city after London and Berlin. The religion that is mainly practiced in Madrid is Roman Catholicism. The city of Madrid is filled with ancient palaces and historical landmarks.The King of Spain resides in Mardrid at the Zarzuela Palace. The President of the Government resides in the Moncloa Palace. This is also where the Council of Ministers meet. Some historical landmarks that still stand in Madrid, specifically in the Almendra Central, hurch of San Jerónimo el Real, the San Nicolás and San Pedro el Viejo church towers, the Bishop's Chapel, the Palace of the Coucnil, Pedro de Ribera etc.

Museums

Madrid is also home to many museums. One museum is the National Archaeological Museum of Madrid,(Museo Arqueológico Nacional), which holds collections of Roman mosaics, Greek ceramics, Islamic art, and Romanesque art. Another museum is the Royal Academy of Fine Arts of San Fernando, (Real Academia de Bellas Artes de San Fernando, which holds fine art collected from the 15th to the 20th centuries, with alumni artists such as Pablo Picasso, Salvador Dalí, Antonio López García, Juan Luna, and Fernando Botero etc. “Madrid.” Wikipedia, Wikimedia Foundation, 28 Nov. 2019,
